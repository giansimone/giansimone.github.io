Who I am.

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Bioengineering at the Imperial College London where I am a member of two amazing groups: the Control Engineering Synthetic Biology group headed by Prof Guy-Bart Stan, and the RLA lab headed by Dr Rodrigo Ledesma-Amaro. Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Systems and Synthetic Biology lab headed by Prof Diego di Bernardo at the Telethon Institute of Genetics and Medicine in Italy. I was awarded a B.Sc in Computer Engineering (Summa cum laude), a M.Sc in Automation Engineering (Summa cum laude), and a Ph.D in Information Technology and Electrical Engineering, all from University of Naples Federico II, Italy, on March 2009, October 2013, and May 2017, respectively.

what I do.

I'm working on a project that uses math to unravel the mysteries behind division of labour in synthetic microbial consortia. I am also studying innovative biomolecular control circuits implemented at host-cell level.
